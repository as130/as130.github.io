My name is Abigail Siegel, but I go by Abby. I was born and raised in New England and I'm currently a senior studying computer science at Brown University with a focus in security. In my free time I like playing the melodica, piano, playing with my dogs, and doing CTFs.

I also really enjoy taking pictures of my dogs!

EXPERIENCE

The summer after my freshman year of college, I interned at The Home Depot corporate offices in Atlanta, Georgia as a software engineering intern on the payments team where I first got introduced to the world of cyber-security. From there, I interned at the Microsoft Corporate offices in Redmond, Washington the summer after my sophomore year as a software engineering intern on the Microsoft Office Feature Blue security team. The summer after my Junior year, I interned at Google in the Sunnyvale, California office (but remote from Providence, Rhode Island!) on the Tech Infra Security and Privacy team as a software engineering intern.

During my time at Brown, I have really enjoyed TAing multiple courses. My Freshman and Sophomore year I was a TA for ENGN0090 (Management of Industrial and Nonprofit Organizations) and ENGN0900 (Managerial Decision Making) and served as the first-ever freshman TA for the courses! My Sophomore Fall I was also a TA for CSCI0170 (Computer Science: An Integrated Introduction), and went on to TA CSCI1660 (Introduction to Computer Systems Security) both my junior spring, and now that I am a senior. I'm also a member of Full Stack at Brown, a club that works on applications for the Brown University community. There, I helped organize our virtual hackathon and the CTF for the hackathon.

I've also been lucky to get to work with ICERM over the years and their Girls Get Math summer program. Back when I was in high school, I got to participate in the program where I was first introduced to computer science, and have since then interned for the program once and served twice as a TA where I created a new lab in which students would create a naive bayesian classifier in MatLab to filter out Spam emails from a set of data.

I have experience working in Python, Typescript, HTML, MatLab, CSS, C, C++, Go, Java, DrRacket, OCaml, Alloy, Forge, x86 Assembly, LaTeX, Git, and Scala.
